Methodology. Each theme's score is a percentile: it places the current reading of that theme's public indicators within their own history (0 = historically low, 50 ≈ around the historical norm, 100 = historically high), oriented so a higher score means the theme's conditions are historically strong. Inputs span the Federal Reserve Economic Data, Yahoo Finance and the World Bank (~85 series). Direction arrows show movement over the past year. This is a monitor, not a forecast — it describes where things sit today and how they have moved, and makes no prediction and gives no investment advice. It is an independent hard-data monitor, not a replication of Deutsche Bank's model: it does not use their dataset, their qualitative or survey inputs, or their ~70-year history, and cannot reproduce their figures. Its thematic structure is inspired by Deutsche Bank Research Institute, Megatrends: AI vs the Decade's Structural Headwinds (May 2026); any quotes from that report are the bank's own views, shown as context.
